## 📄 Overview

<a href="#">Jewelry CAD Dream</a> serves the professional jewelry trade as a tool that bridges the creative intent of designers with the production requirements of manufacturers — producing designs that are not just visually correct but geometrically accurate for the manufacturing processes that will realize them.

The <a href="#">surface modeling tools</a> create the organic curved forms that characterize fine jewelry — flowing shanks, textured surfaces, sculptural elements, and decorative details that require surface modeling capability beyond what primitive-based solid modeling provides. <a href="#">Boolean operations</a> combine, subtract, and intersect solid forms for complex design elements including cutouts, channels, and structural features that define the piece's architecture.

<p align="center">
  <img src="https://jewelrydesignacademy.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/10/jewelry-cad-dream-chennai-designer-training-institute-courses-weekend-classes.jpg" alt="Jewelry CAD Dream screenshot"/>
</p>

<a href="#">Pattern and repeat tools</a> create evenly distributed elements — pave stone arrangements, milgrain borders, filigree patterns — across curved surfaces with mathematically correct spacing and orientation relative to the surface geometry. <a href="#">Assembly workflow</a> combines separately designed components — head, shank, gallery, side stones — into complete assemblies that can be evaluated as unified designs before production.

The <a href="#">dimension and measurement tools</a> verify wall thickness, prong dimensions, stone seat depth, and other critical manufacturing parameters within the design environment before committing to production. <a href="#">Multiple metal material presets</a> visualize the same design in yellow gold, white gold, rose gold, platinum, and silver for client presentation and material selection decisions.
